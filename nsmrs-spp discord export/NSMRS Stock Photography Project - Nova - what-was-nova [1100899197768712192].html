Nova Development Jam 3

Friday Jul. 14 - Sunday Jul. 16, 2023

Hello, and welcome to the 3rd Nova Development Jam! After the success of the first two jams, we've taken a look at what went right and what went wrong to attempt to craft a better experience for everyone.

First, some changes:

  • Projects can now be submitted by teams of no more than 2. This means that you can now work together on your jam projects! Just remember, working in teams may cause unnecessary issues due to communication, and you can only submit one project per person, regardless of team affiliation!
  • The rubric has been adjusted to prioritize growth over strictly quality. We recognize that Nova! has a wide variety of developers of all skill levels, and one of the most common complaints we hear is that it feels impossible to compete against those in the server with previous development experience. (cough, @tryonyx) We've decided to make some changes to the way that projects are judged to ensure that more people have a fair chance at winning, even if the build quality of their project isn't neccesarily better. In the eyes of the new rubric, someone starting out who makes strides to develop their own skills is more likely to win than an experienced developer who cranks out something in 10 minutes. The new categories are Use of Theme, Creativity, Discovery, and X-Factor.
  • Winning projects show a connection to the theme, demonstrate new and original ideas, show that the developers have taken effort to discover new tools and techniques to improve themselves, and have a striking, central X-Factor that makes their project stand out. ### Now, some of the standard NDJ rules:
  • Participants will have 72 hours to build and submit their projects. Once time is up, no submissions will be accepted and any further changes not approved by Astra will disqualify the project.
  • Prizes will include exclusive server roles and Robux* (TBD with Nova!'s current funding)
  • Projects must follow the theme of the jam. The theme will be revealed when the 72 hour timer starts!
  • Projects must be completely original for the jam. Don't submit a previously made project. It goes against the spirit of the jam!
On a personal note, I'd like to thank everyone who has been participating in these dev jams for taking on a new challenge and pushing yourself to learn, have fun, and create magic. To those who have 0 dev experience, consider picking up Roblox Studio at https://create.roblox.com and trying something out! It's never too late to start learning. See you all on Friday! -Astra https://discord.gg/hPAjPKqP?event=1129204490675957831 (edited)
